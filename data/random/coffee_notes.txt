Random Notes on Coffee and Brewing

- Ethiopia Yirgacheffe is still my favorite. Super floral and citrusy. The blueberry notes in the natural process ones are insane.
- Tried a darker roast yesterday, way too bitter for my taste. It tasted like ash and burnt toast. Definitely sticking to light/medium roasts.
- Need to clean the V60 setup, there's some buildup. Maybe get some specialized cleaner like Cafiza.
- Thinking about getting a Comandante grinder, but it's so pricey. The build quality looks amazing though, and the grind consistency is top-tier.
- Cortados are the perfect balance of milk and espresso. Equal parts of both. It's the "real" macchiato if you ask me.
- Should try some cold brew for the summer. Maybe use the Brazilian beans. They usually have nice chocolatey and nutty profiles that work well with cold extraction.
- Coffee shop in the corner has a new single-origin from Colombia (Huila region). Smells like dark chocolate and red apples.
- Don't forget to buy filters on the way back. Need the Hario 02 size.
- Pourover Technique: I've been experimenting with the 4:6 method by Tetsu Kasuya. It really highlights the acidity and sweetness separately.
- Water Quality: I should probably start using Third Wave Water packets. My tap water is a bit too hard and might be masking some of the delicate floral notes.
- AeroPress: Great for traveling. Very forgiving and makes a clean cup. The inverted method is a bit risky but worth it for the body it gives.
- Grind Size: For the V60, I'm aiming for something around the size of kosher salt. Too fine and it stalls; too coarse and it's watery.
- Roasting: Maybe one day I'll get a Hottop roaster and try doing it myself. Green beans are so much cheaper.
